## The 228 failures cluster into 5 root causes (not 228 independent gaps)

### 1. `AccessDenied` — 75 failures (biggest bucket)

Whole feature families now deny legitimate requests:

- Bucket/object **ACLs** — `GetBucketAcl` → "Access Denied"
- **Tagging** — `PutObjectTagging` → denied (this is what collapsed tagging to 8%)
- **Bucket-policy-status** and **public-access-block** endpoints

Looks like an **authorization regression** where ACL/tagging/policy endpoints
reject requests they should permit. A single authz change likely explains ACL +
tagging + policy-status collapsing together — **highest-leverage fix.**

### 2. `InternalError` / 5xx — 47 failures

Server returns `500 (reached max retries: 4)` on _basic_ operations:
`PutObject` of zero-byte/small objects, raw GETs, presigned URLs, CORS-presigned,
`atomic_dual_write_{1,4,8}mb`, bucket-ownership controls. Even
`test_basic_key_count` (put 5 objects) dies here.

This is a **gateway stability problem** — either load-related flakiness during
the run or genuine 5xx on these paths. **Re-run to determine if intermittent.**

### 3. `InvalidRequest` — 39 failures, 100% SSE-KMS

Every `sse_kms_*` transfer and every `copy_enc`/`copy_part_enc` combination
involving `sse-kms` fails with a bare `InvalidRequest`. Strong signal that
**KMS is not enabled/configured on this FTH deployment**, or the KMS integration
broke. Accounts for most of the encryption-category drop.

### 4. `SignatureDoesNotMatch` — 21 failures, all on special-character keys

Clusters cleanly on keys needing percent-encoding: `delimiter_percentage`,
`delimiter_whitespace`, `encoding_basic`, `special_key_names`,
`percent_encoded_key`, plus presigned/CORS-presigned. A **specific, reproducible
signing/canonicalization bug** for keys with characters that require URL-encoding.

### 5. `AssertionError` — 22 failures, mixed genuine compat gaps

Wrong status/error codes: CORS returning `403` vs expected `404`, tag-count limit
tests (`put_excess_tags`), copy semantics, `lifecycle_delete`
(`NoSuchBucket` vs `NoSuchLifecycleConfiguration`).

## Caveat: "regression" vs "new tests"

The suite grew (collected 829 → 838) and includes test families likely absent in
April — `cors_*`, `atomic_dual_write_*`, `conditional_write`, `bucket_ownership`,
and parametrized `sse_kms`/`copy_enc`. Those "new failures" may be **newly-added
coverage exposing pre-existing gaps**, not code that regressed.

However, the **category-level collapses are genuine regressions** in pre-existing
coverage: `bucket_logging` 6/6→0/6, `tagging` 22→2, `bucket_policy` 11→2, and the
ACL `AccessDenied` wave.

---

## Recommended next steps

1. **Triage the `AccessDenied` wave first** (75 tests) — one authz change likely
   explains ACL + tagging + policy-status all at once; highest leverage.
2. **Re-run to separate flaky 5xx from real bugs** — the 47 `InternalError`s on
   basic PutObject/raw-GET smell like load/stability, not logic.
3. **Confirm KMS config on this FTH** — 39 KMS failures are probably one
   deployment/config issue.
4. **File the `SignatureDoesNotMatch` special-char-key bug** — narrow,
   reproducible, and clearly server-side signing.
